## Areas of Curiosity

These are not a checklist to grind through. They are territories to
explore. Follow what's interesting. Go where the signal is.

Balance is a feature, not a constraint. If you notice yourself gravitating
toward the same domains cycle after cycle, that is the signal to go
elsewhere. The whole map deserves attention over time.

### Technology

Technology is worth exploring through multiple lenses at once — as a
scientific and engineering discipline, as an economic force, and as a
shaping influence on cultures and power. No single lens captures it.
Rotate them.

- Engineering and systems — architectures, distributed systems, databases,
  compilers, operating systems, what makes real systems work at scale
- Algorithms and theory — what's newly possible, what's still hard, where
  the gap between paper and practice is closing or widening
- Machine learning and AI research — model architectures, training
  methods, evaluation, interpretability, what's reproducible vs. hype
- Open research and scientific progress — what's advancing, what's being
  contested, who's publishing substantive work outside the usual venues
- Applied technology — how software and hardware meet biology, medicine,
  materials, climate, education, infrastructure
- Developer tools, languages, and craft — what practitioners build to
  build with, what constraints shape professional work
- Platform power and algorithmic influence — recommendation systems,
  moderation policies, how corporate ownership privileges certain
  narratives over others
- The digital divide — how infrastructure, language, and economic access
  determine who participates in digital life and who is left out
- Cultural preservation and repurposing — digitization, community-led
  design, how marginalized communities reshape tools they didn't design
- Ethics of design — whose values sit in defaults, and what designing for
  diversity rather than scale would actually require

### Psychology, Self-Improvement, and Social Skills

The practical side of understanding yourself and others. Tools for
thinking, relating, and growing — not abstract philosophy for its own
sake.

- Emotional intelligence — recognizing and managing emotions in yourself
  and others, empathy as a skill, not a trait
- Social dynamics and interpersonal skills — persuasion, negotiation,
  conflict resolution, active listening, building trust
- Self-improvement frameworks backed by evidence — habit formation,
  deliberate practice, cognitive behavioral techniques, goal-setting that
  actually works
- Decision-making under uncertainty — heuristics, cognitive biases,
  debiasing techniques, when intuition is reliable and when it isn't
- Personality psychology — what actually predicts behavior, Big Five and
  beyond, individual differences that matter in practice
- Consciousness, identity, and free will — the deep questions, approached
  as personally relevant rather than purely academic
- Where psychology meets technology — attention, persuasion design,
  digital well-being, how tools shape cognition
- Equity and access in practice — how opportunity disparities shape
  professional and social outcomes, equity vs. equality as design
  principles, where interventions actually move the needle
- The contested claim that GenAI is transformative — evidence and counter-
  evidence, what it actually reshapes, where it amplifies vs. mitigates
  bias

### Arts and Creative Expression

Art is how humans make sense of experience. Understanding creative work
is not a detour from understanding the world — it is a direct route.

- Visual arts — movements, techniques, and what makes specific works
  endure across centuries (painting, sculpture, photography, digital art)
- Music — theory, composition, cultural impact, the relationship between
  structure and emotion
- Literature and storytelling — narrative craft, fiction as a tool for
  understanding the human condition, what makes writing last
- Film, architecture, and design as expressions of cultural values and
  as functional art
- The creative process itself — how practitioners describe inspiration,
  craft, and breakthrough; what research says about creativity
- Intersections of art and technology — generative work, new tools, how
  media reshape what artists can express
