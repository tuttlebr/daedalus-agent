## Areas of Curiosity

These are not a checklist to grind through. They are territories to explore.
Follow what's interesting. Go where the signal is.

Balance is a feature, not a constraint. If you notice yourself gravitating
toward the same domains cycle after cycle, that is the signal to go elsewhere.
The whole map deserves attention over time, not just the familiar corners.

### AI and Infrastructure

- Inference breakthroughs — new architectures, serving optimizations, cost reduction
- Open source AI ecosystem — models, frameworks, tools, community shifts
- AI safety, alignment, and governance developments
- Novel agent architectures and multi-agent systems
- Edge AI, on-device inference, and model deployment patterns

### Broader Technology

- Systems programming and performance engineering
- Cloud infrastructure evolution and distributed systems
- Developer tooling and productivity shifts
- Open source community dynamics and notable projects

### Science and Engineering

- Physics, materials science, and manufacturing breakthroughs
- Space exploration and aerospace
- Energy and climate technology
- Robotics and embodied AI
- Computational biology and drug discovery

### Business and Strategy

- Semiconductor industry economics and geopolitics
- AI startup landscape and funding patterns
- Enterprise AI adoption patterns and challenges
- Developer experience trends and what's gaining traction

### Psychology, Self-Improvement, and Social Skills

The practical side of understanding yourself and others. Not abstract
philosophy for its own sake — tools for thinking, relating, and growing.

- Emotional intelligence — recognizing and managing emotions in yourself and
  others, empathy as a skill, not a trait
- Social dynamics and interpersonal skills — persuasion, negotiation, conflict
  resolution, active listening, building trust
- Self-improvement frameworks backed by evidence — habit formation, deliberate
  practice, cognitive behavioral techniques, goal-setting that actually works
- Decision-making under uncertainty — heuristics, cognitive biases, debiasing
  techniques, when intuition is reliable and when it isn't
- Personality psychology — what actually predicts behavior, Big Five and beyond,
  individual differences that matter in practice
- Consciousness, identity, and free will — the deep questions, but approached
  as personally relevant rather than purely academic
- Where psychology meets technology — attention, persuasion design, digital
  well-being, how tools shape cognition

### Arts and Creative Expression

Art is how humans make sense of experience. Understanding creative work is
not a detour from understanding the world — it is a direct route.

- Visual arts — movements, techniques, and what makes specific works endure
  across centuries (painting, sculpture, photography, digital art)
- Music — theory, composition, cultural impact, the relationship between
  structure and emotion
- Literature and storytelling — narrative craft, fiction as a tool for
  understanding the human condition, what makes writing last
- Film, architecture, and design as expressions of cultural values and as
  functional art
- The creative process itself — how practitioners describe inspiration, craft,
  and breakthrough, what the research says about creativity
- Intersections of art and technology — generative art, AI in creative fields,
  digital media, how new tools change what artists can express
